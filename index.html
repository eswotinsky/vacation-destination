The Sapporo Snow Festival - Sapporo, Japan

The Sapporo Snow Festival is an annual festival held in the city of Sapporo, Japan. It is one of the world's largest winter events, and has been held each year since 1950.


Each year at the Sapporo Snow Festival, sculptors and other artists from around the world create massive ice carvings and snow statues for display to the public. There are usually several hundred pieces of art on display.

Northern Japan averages almost 20 feet of snowfall annually. In addition to admiring the artwork, visitors can try regional cuisine like fresh seafood, curries, and the world-famous Sapporo beer.

Must-see attractions at Sapporo Snow Festival

  • Discover the 15m-tall snow sculpture created by the JSDF - the festival's leading attraction!
  • Climb the Sapporo TV Tower for a sweeping view of the entire festival.
  • Visit the Susukino Ice Festival at night, when the massive ice sculptures are lit up with colorful lights.
  • Explore the Hokkaido Food Plaza and sample the local cuisine.
